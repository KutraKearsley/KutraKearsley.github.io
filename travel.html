Getting to Flagstaff

Flagstaff does have a small airport (FLG), but unfortunately the only places that it offers direct flights to are Phoenix (PHX), and Dallas (DFW). So if you fly into Flagstaff you'll be connecting. But the airport is small (i.e. not busy) and reasonably close to town so its not a bad choice.

Another (typically less expensive) option is to fly to Phoenix and then take a 2.5 hour shuttle to Flagstaff. We use Groome and have had good experiences with it. You can choose to be dropped off on NAU Campus or at the Amtrak station downtown.

Accomodation

Hotels

Flagstaff has a tonne of hotels. Our reception is at the Weatherford hotel , which has both regular rooms as well as "historically preserved rooms" (i.e. no private bathrooms but still nice). There are other standard hotels, such as the Mariott within walking distance of the Weatherford.

If you're looking for a fun option, you might want to check out the Hotel Monte Vista which is just down the street from the reception. There is also the Model DuBeau which is very cute and has lots of parking if you are driving into town or renting a car.

Camping

If you're looking to do some camping while you're in town, there are a tonne of free spots thanks to all the National Forest land. Feel free to ask us if you're looking for a spot in a specific area.

Things to do!

Near Flagstaff

Grand Canyon National Park: A no-brainer. 1.5 hours northwest of Flagstaff. There is also a Groome shuttle that will take you there (but they fill up quickly so if you want to do that book early!). Our favourite short hike is out to Shoshone Point, and if you want to see where we got engaged, you can hike down the Hermit Trail to windy point. The park has free busses that run all day in the park to get to and from different trails and viewpoints. If you need recommendations for long hikes let us know!

National Monuments: Flagstaff has 3 national monuments within a half hour drive: Sunset Crater (a dormant volcano and lava field), Walnut Canyon (a gold mine of Ancestral Puebloan archeological sites), and Wupatki (a massive Ancestral Puebloan settlement site). All are great half day activities.

Sedona: A 45 minute drive through beautiful Oak Creek Canyon brings you to Sedona's famous red rocks. Lots of great hikes, we like the Hangover Trail, but beware the traffic and trails can get pretty busy. Most popular hikes now require a Red Rock Pass to park at trailheads. The town is cute too!

In Flagstaff

Museum of Northern Arizona: A Flagstaff Institution! Learn about ancient civilizations and modern native people in the Southwest. Also see what the San Francisco Peaks looked like before they blew up.

First Friday Art Walk: Happening on the Friday before the wedding (September 6th). A monthly art walk in Downtown Flagstaff where gallaries near San Francisco and Aspen Street open their doors in the evening from 5-9pm.

Farmers Market: Happens on Sunday mornings from 8am to noon in downtown.

Lowell Observatory: Taylor works here (and might be able to snag you a free pass if you give her a heads up)! They have nightly stargazing through lots of great telescopes.

Breweries: There are a ton of breweries all within walking distance of Downtown. Our favourites are Mother Road Brewing, Dark Sky Brewing, and Historic Brewing.

Buffalo Park: Great place for a short afternoon walk!
